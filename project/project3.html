Third Mug Coffee Ordering UI

Concept Project · UI/UX Design

Project Background

A minimalistic mobile ordering experience designed for a fictional coffee brand, "Third Mug." The focus is on clean UI, intuitive interaction, and seamless user flow in a mobile context.

Problem Statement

Many coffee shop apps are cluttered and overwhelming for users who just want to place a quick order. This concept solves that by simplifying the ordering process—removing distractions and guiding users through a straightforward, focused experience.

My Role

UI Designer — handled wireframing, prototyping, and visual design of the ordering flow.

Accessibility Considerations

I used a high contrast between text and background to maintain readability in any lighting. Fonts are large and clean to support quick scanning. Interactive elements are finger-friendly in size. Visual clutter was intentionally avoided to reduce cognitive load.

UX Considerations

The core focus was on reducing user effort in a mobile ordering flow. I prioritized large, tappable elements for easy thumb use, a linear ordering path to eliminate confusion, and clear confirmation states. Only the essential screens were included, removing clutter and letting users complete an order in just a few taps. Icons and labels were paired to support both recognition and clarity.

Next Steps

I’d like to test the prototype with real users, especially commuters or on-the-go coffee drinkers, to validate flow efficiency and identify pain points. Potential features to explore include loyalty rewards and order history.

Reflection

This concept challenged me to remove all non-essential elements while still creating a delightful ordering experience. I focused on what matters most—clarity, ease, and flow—and learned how intentional simplicity improves usability.
